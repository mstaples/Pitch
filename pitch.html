Introduction

Brunelleschi: Age of Architects (Brune) is an exciting online multiplayer game currently under development and on track to launch this fall.

The online gaming market is massive, with an enthusiastic global audience which is both growing all the time and constantly hungry for new gaming experiences.

Introduction

The two biggest obstacles to new products in this market are the challenges of lengthy development times and the ability to self sustain after launch long enough for some of the many gaming communities to find and build a home in the new virtual environment.

Introduction

Brune has already conquered much of the first of these challenges, with the game's engine currently at more than 90% completion, a web client similarly near completion, and with mobile, graphic (PC and console), and VR clients already in playable pre alpha phases of development.

Introduction

Current products in the market focus on initial launch income rather than sustainable long term play, meaning games are rushed to market as mere accessories to their advertising campaigns, and leaving customers craving innovative game play perpetually dissatisfied.

In contrast, Brune offers a rich depth and variety of experience to players.

Expanded Information

Brune is built on a foundation of open source software. Using cutting edge components such as the Symfony2 framework, PHP5 (with plans to upgrade to PHP7 at launch), mediawiki, phpbb, and the open source community Brune has been able to create innovative game play experiences using a minimal budget and small development team.

This, combined with automatically expanding and contracting server usage through Digital Ocean or a similar service, will allow us to continue to improve, support, and maintain the product after launch with sustainably low overhead.

Expanded Information

A game which attracts and serves some of the countless online gaming communities can be a lasting success. Many such games see millions of players returning each month.

Expanded Information

Brune features a variety of features to attract and facilitate such communities within the game. Communication and shared storytelling are made easy through integrated forums, mediawiki, and game messaging.

Characters within the game are able to form relationships, groups, and even coalitions between their settlements as they work together to build steadily expanding civilizations.

Expanded Information

By facilitating these extant communities to take root in Brune, we accelerate the rate at which new players discover the product and increase the amount of time each player will remain engaged.

Expanded Information

One of the key elements to sustainable human happiness is satisfying the desire for self improvement. Games which allow for the passive absorption of real world concepts through gameplay have long been a favorite among gamers.

Brune accommodates this healthy human desire by offering fun interactive play rich with vocabulary, mathematics, and concepts from history, civics, tactics, economics, and more.


While never asking players to stop and solve a math problem or pass a pop quiz, simply playing the game rewards players with exposure to interesting ideas and real world mechanics, granting that precious sense of satisfaction that comes from learning new things.

Expanded Information

For Example: Harvesting grain at a Farm, owning a Bakery which produces bread from grain, and pricing Bread sales on the markets for other players to purchase to eat for food bonuses or to sustain their troops are each simple fun interactions any player can have, but they also illustrate a production chain in an interactive way which leads to passively accumulated understanding.

Brune provides players a vast array of opportunities to explore, shape, and learn from their shared virtual world.
